Frequently asked questions

Answers to most common questions.

What is blockchain technology?

Blockchain technology is a decentralized digital ledger system that records transactions across multiple computers.

How does blockchain enhance transparency in public record management?

Additional layers of encryption can be added to the data stored on the blockchain, further enhancing security and confidentiality.

How does blockchain protect the confidentiality of public records?

Blockchain is decentralized, meaning data is stored across a network of computers (nodes). This makes it extremely difficult for any single entity to alter or access the records without consensus from the majority of the network.

Is blockchain technology secure against cyber threats and hacking attempts?

Yes, blockchain is highly secure due to its decentralized nature and cryptographic algorithms. Hacking a blockchain network is extremely challenging and would require a significant amount of computational power.

Can public records be accessed and verified by third parties using blockchain?

Yes, with the appropriate permissions and cryptographic keys, third parties can access and verify public records on the blockchain, ensuring trust and authenticity.

How does blockchain technology handle privacy concerns associated with public records?

Blockchain ensures privacy through encryption. Sensitive information is stored securely, and access is restricted to authorized parties, maintaining confidentiality.
